# Voice Profile

This file should contain your personal writing voice profile — a document that captures how you actually communicate so Claude can match your style when drafting messages, emails, and documents on your behalf.

## How to Generate Your Voice Profile

The most effective approach is to have Claude analyze your real communication patterns:

1. **Gather source material.** Point Claude at your last 30 days of outgoing emails and Slack messages. The more varied the audience (direct reports, peers, executives, external partners), the richer the profile.

2. **Ask Claude to extract patterns.** Have it analyze:
   - Sentence structure and length
   - Vocabulary and preferred phrases
   - Formality level by audience
   - How you open and close messages
   - How you deliver feedback, bad news, good news
   - Rhetorical patterns (how you frame decisions, make requests)
   - Quirks (lowercase in Slack? Multi-message style? Emoji usage?)

3. **Review and refine.** The first draft will capture the broad strokes. Read it and correct anything that feels off. Run it again with corrections as feedback.

4. **Reference it in your CLAUDE.md.** Add a line to your global/CLAUDE.md Communication Style section:
   `See context/voice-profile.md for detailed writing voice reference when drafting external communications.`

## What a Good Voice Profile Covers

- Patterns by channel (Slack DM vs email vs document vs meeting)
- Patterns by audience (direct reports vs peers vs executives vs external)
- Key vocabulary and phrases you use frequently
- Things you never say or actively avoid
- How you handle specific situations (giving feedback, making requests, saying no)
